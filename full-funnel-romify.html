The seam — where the funnel breaks
This is the whole point of reading both lenses together.
The through-line
Romify captures demand efficiently at events — that's not the problem. The problem is the handoff: 79% of captured demand decays in the CRM before sales ever works it, because there's no MQL/nurture layer to catch it. That dark database is the upstream reservoir that the sales assessment sees downstream as a $2.83M cold pipeline and past-due deals. Same demand, two symptoms. The $878K of active cold pipeline on the sales side is a slice of the 9,423 dark contacts on the marketing side.
Confirmed independently from the sales side
Romify's own closed-lost data proves the same point. Of 477 lost deals ($3.92M), 86% carry a logged reason — and the single largest cluster is silence: 74 deals worth $686,500 marked unresponsive, ghosted, or no-contact. Price/budget is a close second (63 deals / $549,150), but only 5 deals anywhere mention ROI or value — the value case largely isn't being made, so price becomes the stated reason by default. Deals are not mostly dying on price. They are dying of non-engagement, the same disease as the 9,423 cold contacts upstream.
The reassuring half: once demand reaches a pilot it converts at 77%, and renewals run 84%. The engine works. The leak is entirely at the top — captured demand that never gets worked.
3
The one fix that moves the whole funnel
Because the two problems are one problem, one motion addresses both ends.
Fix upstream (marketing)
A reactivation cadence on the 9,423 dark contacts, prioritized by fit and event recency. Stand up an MQL layer so demand quality becomes scoreable and only worked leads reach sales.
Effect downstream (sales)
The cold pipeline shrinks at the source, more qualified demand reaches the 77%-converting pilot stage, and reps stop working a database that was dead on arrival. The demo-to-pilot wall gets fed better inputs.
One motion, whole funnel.
Cortex orchestrates the reactivation cadence and the MQL layer above your existing HubSpot — read-only, every external action human-approved. The marketing baseline and the sales benchmark become the scoreboard we move together, quarter over quarter.
